# Build-in-Public — Weekly Friday Numbers Post

Per the IH front-page pattern (research saved Apr 2026): build-in-public
"Week N: numbers" posts are the most-rewarded format on Indie Hackers
right now. Same pattern works on Twitter/X threads and r/Entrepreneur.

**Cadence**: every Friday, ~5pm Pacific (peak IH evening traffic).

**Length**: 200–400 words. Resist the urge to write more — it kills
engagement. The numbers do the work.

---

## Template (fill in, post)

> # Gotta Go — Week {N}
>
> _Free public-bathroom finder I'm building solo. Live at
> https://oscar-leung.github.io/restroom-finder/_
>
> ## What I shipped this week
> - {3 bullet points, 1 line each. Be specific.}
> - {e.g. "Real walking-route polylines via OSRM (free)"}
> - {e.g. "Search any address worldwide via Nominatim"}
>
> ## Numbers
> | Metric | This week | Last week | Δ |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | Sessions | {N} | {N} | {±%} |
> | GO clicks | {N} | {N} | {±%} |
> | Bathrooms in DB | {N} | {N} | {±} |
> | Reviews submitted | {N} | {N} | {±} |
> | Tip jar ($) | ${N} | ${N} | {±} |
>
> ## What's working
> - {one specific thing — e.g. "List-first hero outperforms map-first"}
>
> ## What's not
> - {one honest thing — e.g. "iOS install funnel still sub-3%"}
>
> ## Next week
> - {one shippable goal}
>
> Roast it, ask anything, tell me what you'd build differently.

## Where to post (in order, ~10 min total)

1. **Indie Hackers** → https://www.indiehackers.com/post/new — group: "Building"
2. **r/Entrepreneur** — same body, different intro line
3. **Twitter/X thread** — break the table into 5 tweets:
   1. The TL;DR + URL
   2. What shipped (3 bullets)
   3. Numbers screenshot
   4. What's working / what's not
   5. Next week ask + thank-you

## Tracking

Every URL gets:
```
?utm_source={platform}&utm_medium=bip-week-{N}
```

So if Friday post drives 80 sessions on IH and 200 on Twitter, the
analytics-agent sees it Saturday morning and tells you which platform
to lean into next week.

## Fail mode to avoid

If you have **no numbers** to share (zero sessions, zero GO clicks),
DON'T post. The format requires real data; faking it tanks credibility.
Skip a Friday and post when you have the launch posts driving traffic
in week 2.

## What "good" looks like

| Week | Sessions | GO clicks | Notes |
|------|----------|-----------|-------|
| Pre-launch | <50 | <10 | Just you + Claude testing |
| Launch week | 500–2,000 | 100–500 | PH + Reddit posts hit |
| Week 4 | 100–300 | 30–80 | Most launch traffic gone, organic builds |
| Week 12 | 1,000+ | 300+ | SEO + retention compounding |
| Week 26 | 5,000+ | 1,500+ | One viral moment + steady SEO |

Post the numbers honestly. People reward "Week 3, 87 sessions, here's
what I'm trying" more than "Week 3, 8,400 sessions" without proof.
